## üáÆüá≥ **Indian Legal Compliance**

### **Primary Legislation:**

#### **1. Copyright Act, 1957** (as amended 2012)

- **Section 13**: Original literary works protected
- **Section 14**: Rights of owner (reproduction, adaptation, distribution)
- **Section 51**: Copyright infringement definition
- **Section 52**: Fair dealing provisions
- **Section 57**: Moral rights of author
- **Section 63**: Penalties for infringement

**Registration:**

- Office: Copyright Office, Mumbai
- Application: Statement of particulars + fee
- Processing: 4-6 months (approx)
- Certificate: Lifetime + 60 years

#### **2. Trade Marks Act, 1999** (as amended 2010, 2017)

- **Section 9**: Absolute grounds for refusal
- **Section 11**: Relative grounds for refusal
- **Section 28**: Rights conferred by registration
- **Section 29**: Infringement of registered trademarks
- **Section 103-109**: Penalties and offenses

**Registration:**

- Registry: Trade Marks Registry (Mumbai/Delhi/Chennai/Kolkata)
- Classes: Nice Classification (9, 35, 42)
- Processing: 18-24 months
- Renewal: Every 10 years

#### **3. Information Technology Act, 2000** (as amended 2008)

- **Section 43**: Penalty for damage to computer systems
- **Section 66**: Computer-related offenses
- **Section 66B**: Receiving stolen computer resource
- **Section 66C**: Identity theft
- **Section 66D**: Cheating by personation
- **Section 72**: Breach of confidentiality and privacy

**Compliance:**

- Digital signatures valid
- Electronic contracts enforceable
- Data protection mandatory
- Cybersecurity measures required

#### **4. Patents Act, 1970** (as amended 2005)

- Protection for inventive algorithms (if novel)
- Software per se not patentable (Section 3(k))
- Technical effect + hardware = patentable
- Duration: 20 years from filing date

## üåç **International Protection**

### **Treaties and Conventions:**

#### **1. Berne Convention** (India joined 1928)

- Automatic protection in 179 countries
- No registration required
- Minimum protection: Life + 50 years
- India provides Life + 60 years

#### **2. WIPO Copyright Treaty (WCT)**

- Digital rights protection
- Anti-circumvention provisions
- Rights management information
- Online distribution rights

#### **3. TRIPS Agreement** (India member since 1995)

- WTO framework
- Minimum IP standards
- Enforcement mechanisms
- Dispute resolution

#### **4. Madrid Protocol** (Trademarks)

- Single application for multiple countries
- Cost-effective international registration
- Covers 130+ countries
- Managed by WIPO

#### **5. Paris Convention** (Industrial Property)

- Priority rights (12 months for trademarks)
- National treatment principle
- Right of priority
- Protection against unfair competition
